About

Old Dog · New Tricks

The adage goes that you can't teach an old dog new tricks — but here we are, learning coding and apps at 60.

The Story

Seven months ago, I ran a construction company. We were doing well. I took on a contract in a coastal town. And then, without any signs or warning, everything just fell apart. We ended up losing it all — back to rock bottom.

After a period of deep depression and having nowhere to turn, I was doing research on Business systems for Back Office Management — BPO outsourcing — when I came across a site offering training in podcasting. After doing the boot camp, I realized that as a novice, there were other people like me who didn't have the wherewithal to do podcasting. So out of that was born the setting up of a podcasting studio dashboard — first for myself, and then I soon realized the potential of marketing modules for the greater market.

The 9Count

"9Count" is my reference to a boxing and fighting analogy. I've been down on the canvas with everyone thinking that it's all over — no fight left in me. Yet at the 9-count, I get up, go to my corner, dust off and wash the blood away, and then come back out swinging. (At the count of 10, you are deemed to have lost.)

That's where both names derived from: Old Dog Systems — because you can't teach an old dog new tricks, but here I am at 60 learning coding and apps. And 9Count Systems — because we got back up when everyone thought we were done.

What We Build

Rough Diamond Studio is the result of many iterations, close to being finalized for alpha testing. Other modular systems for small businesses are also in the initial concept stage, but nothing to proof of concept yet.

We build for operators who've been there — tools, systems, and content that help you get back up, dust off, and come back swinging.
